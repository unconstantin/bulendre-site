Objective

This analysis examines customer purchasing patterns in an e-commerce dataset to identify key behavior segments and uncover opportunities for targeted marketing strategies. The goal is to understand what drives customer value and how to optimize retention efforts.

Dataset

The dataset contains transaction records from an online retail store, including customer IDs, purchase dates, product information, and transaction values.

üìä Source: Kaggle E-Commerce Dataset

üìù Records: 50,000+ transactions

üìÜ Period: January 2023 - December 2023

Main Insights

  • Revenue Concentration: High-value customers (top 20%) contribute 65% of total revenue
  • Peak Activity: Purchasing activity peaks between 10 AM and 2 PM on weekdays
  • Retention Challenge: Customer retention rate drops significantly after the first 90 days
  • Seasonal Patterns: Product categories show distinct seasonal patterns in demand
  • Cart Abandonment: Highest abandonment rate for items priced between $50-$100

Conclusions

The analysis reveals clear customer segmentation opportunities. Implementing targeted retention strategies for new customers and optimizing product recommendations for high-value segments could significantly improve overall revenue.

Key recommendations include developing a loyalty program for high-value customers, implementing abandoned cart recovery campaigns for mid-price items, and creating targeted onboarding sequences to improve 90-day retention rates.

Next Steps: Further investigation into cart abandonment causes and seasonal demand patterns is recommended for developing more precise marketing campaigns.
